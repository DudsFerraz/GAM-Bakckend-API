# GAM Piracicaba

> Portuguese version: [gam-piracicaba.pt-BR.md](gam-piracicaba.pt-BR.md)  
> Software project: [GAM Piracicaba API](../../README.md)

GAM Piracicaba, the Missionary Animation Group of Piracicaba, is a Salesian youth group connected to the Salesian Youth Movement. Its identity is rooted in a simple and demanding conviction: young people can evangelize, educate, serve, and build community when they are formed through responsibility, friendship, faith, and mission.

The group brings together young adults who have already lived meaningful pastoral experiences and want to continue their Salesian journey beyond school age. Many members are former students or pastoral leaders from the Dom Bosco schools in Piracicaba. As they begin to shape their own life projects, they also choose to remain close to children, teenagers, families, and communities that need presence, care, and hope.

## Salesian Roots

The Salesians are a Catholic religious congregation founded by Saint John Bosco in 1859. Their mission is centered on the evangelization and education of young people, especially those facing social vulnerability.

In the Salesian tradition, education and evangelization are not separate paths. To educate is also to welcome, accompany, protect, and open possibilities. To evangelize is also to create spaces where young people can experience joy, belonging, responsibility, prayer, and service.

GAM Piracicaba lives this spirituality in a concrete way: through Oratorio, missionary visits, celebrations, formation, volunteer work, and a strong commitment to being present among children and young people.

## History

The first GAM was born in Piracicaba in 2004. It began as a group of former students from the Dom Bosco schools who wanted to continue living the Salesian charism after their school years.

Over time, the group's activities grew and inspired the creation of other GAM groups in cities across the state of Sao Paulo. Today, more than a dozen GAM groups are connected to the Salesian Province of Sao Paulo, each one carrying the same missionary spirit in its own local reality.

GAM Piracicaba remains the original group and continues to be an important reference for this path.

## Life of the Group

GAM Piracicaba has around 60 active members and carries out activities in Piracicaba and in other cities in the state of Sao Paulo.

The group is connected to local pastoral work and to the broader provincial youth ministry. Its members act with a level of autonomy, but always within the Salesian mission and in dialogue with the communities and institutions they serve.

Its main activities include:

- weekly Oratorio with children and teenagers;
- annual missionary action in another community;
- support for missionary weeks organized by the Dom Bosco schools;
- moments of prayer, celebration, formation, and volunteer service;
- social initiatives connected to the needs of the families reached by the group.

## Oratorio

The Oratorio is one of the central expressions of the Salesian mission. In Piracicaba, it usually happens on Saturday afternoons at Colegio Dom Bosco Sao Mario.

Children and teenagers, many of them from lower-income families, are welcomed for an afternoon of recreation, friendship, and care. The activities may include games, sports, face painting, drawing, group dynamics, and other forms of simple, joyful entertainment.

The most important moment of the day is the `Boa Tarde`, a Salesian tradition inspired by Mamma Margaret, Don Bosco's mother. At the end of the Oratorio, the children and teenagers gather with the volunteers for a short moment of welcome, prayer, reflection, and encouragement.

The Oratorio is not only an event. It is a place of relationship. Week after week, it creates trust between volunteers, children, teenagers, and families.

## Missionary Action

Once a year, GAM Piracicaba carries out a missionary action that usually lasts from seven to nine days. During this period, the group travels to another city or community and lives a concentrated experience of service, prayer, evangelization, and fraternity.

The missionary routine is usually organized around three moments:

| Time | Activity | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Morning | House blessings | Missionaries visit families in small groups, offering prayer, conversation, listening, and reflection. |
| Afternoon | Oratorio and workshops | Children and teenagers take part in recreational activities; adults may be invited to simple workshops or community activities. |
| Evening | Celebration | The group prepares moments such as the Salesian rosary, celebration of forgiveness, celebration of light, Stations of the Cross, and other prayer experiences. |

The missionary action is one of the clearest signs of the group's identity. It asks members to leave their usual routine, live with simplicity, work together, and meet each community with respect.

## Missionary Week

The Missionary Week is organized by the Dom Bosco schools in Piracicaba and follows a format similar to GAM's annual missionary action.

Although it is not an activity created or led by GAM Piracicaba, the group contributes through the presence of members who are individually invited by the schools to help with organization and execution. Their role is especially valuable because they bring previous missionary experience and can support students who are still discovering this way of serving.

This connection also shows one of GAM's most important roles: helping young people continue growing in Salesian life after their school years, while also supporting younger students in their first missionary experiences.

## Why This Software Project Exists

The GAM Piracicaba software project was born from the needs of this volunteer reality. A group that welcomes children, organizes events, forms missionaries, records presences, coordinates responsibilities, and serves communities needs tools that respect its mission and make its work easier to sustain.

The system is being developed voluntarily as a practical contribution to the group. Its purpose is to help organize information, reduce manual work, protect continuity, and allow volunteers to focus more fully on the people and communities they serve.
